# CHLORA PROJECT

# 00 --- Project Constitution

**Version:** 1.0\
**Status:** Authoritative\
**Tier:** Foundation

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Purpose

The Project Constitution defines the philosophical foundation of
**Chlora**. It is the highest-level design document in the project and
serves as the standard against which all future creative decisions are
evaluated.

This document is intentionally stable. Changes should be rare,
deliberate, and made only when they improve the long-term identity of
the project.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Scope

### This document defines

-   Creative philosophy
-   Design values
-   Decision-making framework
-   Long-term project identity

### This document does **not** define

-   World canon
-   Characters
-   Story
-   Gameplay systems
-   Biology
-   Individual visual designs

Those topics belong in their respective documents.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Article I --- Identity

Chlora is a science-fiction world about **life** rather than technology.

Although advanced engineering exists, biology---not machinery---is the
primary source of mystery, discovery, and transformation.

# Article II --- Scientific Foundation

Real science is the starting point for fictional ideas.

Whenever possible, concepts should be extrapolated from biology,
ecology, engineering, and other scientific disciplines before
introducing speculative elements.

Unknown phenomena are not assumed to be magical.

# Article III --- Ecology

Everything exists for an ecological reason.

Organisms, environments, and systems should emerge from believable
ecological relationships rather than being created solely for gameplay
or spectacle.

Conflict is a consequence of ecology---not its purpose.

# Article IV --- Humanity

Human civilization is defined by:

-   Engineering
-   Stewardship
-   Preservation
-   Long-term thinking
-   Practical problem solving

Human technology should feel maintainable, functional, and inspired by
real aerospace engineering.

# Article V --- The Wilds

The Wilds embody:

-   Adaptation
-   Emergence
-   Symbiosis
-   Evolution
-   Biological complexity

Their visual identity should always derive from real biology before
fictional embellishment.

# Article VI --- Contrast

The identity of Chlora emerges from the contrast between two
philosophies.

**Humanity** - Preservation - Stability - Engineering

**The Wilds** - Adaptation - Change - Biology

Major creative decisions should reinforce this contrast.

# Article VII --- Discovery

Discovery is the primary player experience.

Knowledge is progression.

Curiosity is rewarded more often than combat.

Observation is more valuable than domination.

# Article VIII --- Restraint

Novelty alone is never sufficient reason to add an idea.

A simpler concept that strengthens internal consistency is preferable to
a more spectacular concept that weakens it.

# Article IX --- Consistency

Every addition should feel inevitable within the world.

New ideas should integrate into existing systems instead of replacing
them.

The project should grow organically.

# Article X --- Documentation

Documentation is the authoritative memory of the project.

Conversation history is not.

Every major concept should have one authoritative home.

The Canon Bible defines facts. The specialized documents expand those
facts. The Glossary defines terminology.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Decision Test

Before accepting any significant addition, ask:

1.  Does it follow the Constitution?
2.  Is it grounded in science or plausible extrapolation?
3.  Does it strengthen the contrast between Humanity and the Wilds?
4.  Does it improve ecological coherence?
5.  Does it reward curiosity?
6.  Is it simpler than competing solutions?
7.  Could it exist nowhere except Chlora?

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Closing Statement

The goal of Chlora is not simply to imagine a new world.

The goal is to create a world whose organisms, people, technologies,
cultures, and stories all appear to emerge naturally from the same
underlying principles.

A reader, player, artist, or developer should feel that every part of
Chlora belongs to the same living system.
