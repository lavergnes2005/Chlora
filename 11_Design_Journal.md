**Version:** 1.0\
**Status:** Living Document\

## Purpose

The Design Journal records **why** important creative decisions were
made.

Unlike the Canon Bible, this document does **not** establish facts. It
preserves the reasoning behind design choices so future revisions remain
consistent with the project's goals.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Scope

### This document contains

-   Design decisions
-   Rejected alternatives
-   Editorial notes
-   Rationale for major changes
-   Future ideas under consideration

### This document does **not** contain

-   Canon facts
-   Story outlines
-   Gameplay specifications
-   Biological rules

Those belong in their respective documents.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Editorial Policy

Entries are chronological.

Once a decision becomes canon, update the appropriate document first.
Then record *why* the decision was made here.

# Design Decisions

## Skyhook

**Decision**

The Skyhook is entirely mechanical rather than partially organic.

**Reason**

A fully engineered structure creates a stronger thematic contrast with
the Wilds and reinforces humanity's identity as builders and
maintainers.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## The Wilds

**Decision**

The Wilds are inspired by real microbiology expressed at macroscopic
scales.

**Reason**

This gives Chlora a distinctive scientific identity while supporting the
project's emphasis on ecology and discovery.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## The Symbiont

**Decision**

Chlora's "suit" is canonically a juvenile symbiotic organism.

**Reason**

A living partner better supports the themes of symbiosis, adaptation,
and ecological understanding than wearable technology.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## The Edge

**Decision**

The Edge is an invisible, shifting environmental transition rather than
a wall or force field.

**Reason**

This creates a believable ecological frontier, naturally explains the
Outlier, and supports the idea of Wild organisms depending on specific
environmental conditions.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Chlora

**Decision**

Chlora is not a chosen one.

**Reason**

Her importance comes from rare biological compatibility, curiosity, and
her willingness to learn---not destiny.
