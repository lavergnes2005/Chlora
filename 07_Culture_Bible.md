## Purpose

The Culture Bible defines how human civilization functions in Chlora.

This document explains the shared values, institutions, traditions, and
daily life of the people who inhabit the Skyhook. Individual people
belong in the Character Bible.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### This document defines

-   Institutions

# Cultural Foundation

Skyhook civilization is built around a single guiding ideal:

**Preservation.**

Preservation is not resistance to progress. It is the belief that
civilization survives only through careful stewardship across
generations.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Core Values

-   Stewardship
-   Responsibility
-   Competence
-   Scientific curiosity
-   Cooperation
-   Long-term thinking
-   Maintenance over replacement

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# The Frozen

The Frozen provide continuity across generations.

They are respected as living repositories of institutional memory and
long-term planning rather than as infallible figures.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# The Custodians

Custodians represent the cultural ideal of service.

Maintaining infrastructure, archives, and life-support is regarded as
honorable work because civilization depends upon continual care.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Education

Education emphasizes:

-   Engineering
-   Biology
-   Systems thinking
-   Scientific observation
-   History
-   Practical maintenance

Children are taught that every generation inherits the responsibility to
preserve the Skyhook for the next.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Research

Scientific inquiry is encouraged.

Knowledge is considered a shared resource that should outlive any
individual researcher.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Relationship with the Wilds

The Wilds are viewed with respect rather than simple fear.

They are understood to be dangerous, valuable, and worthy of careful
study.

Exploration favors observation over exploitation.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Daily Life

Common characteristics include:

-   Functional clothing
-   Shared public spaces
-   Repair culture
-   Practical engineering
-   Efficient use of resources
-   Strong institutional memory
