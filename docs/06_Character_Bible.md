## Purpose

The Character Bible defines the major individuals and organizations that
inhabit the world of Chlora.

This document focuses on **who people are**. Social structures, customs,
and institutions belong in the Culture Bible.

# Character Design Philosophy

Characters should feel like products of their environment rather than
archetypes.

Curiosity, competence, empathy, and adaptability are valued over destiny
or prophecy.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Chlora

## Role

-   Protagonist
-   Bridge between humanity and the Wilds

## Established Facts

-   Forms a rare partnership with a juvenile symbiont.
-   Is not chosen by destiny.
-   Becomes important through biological compatibility and her
    willingness to learn.

## Personality Foundation

-   Curious
-   Observant
-   Practical
-   Compassionate
-   Persistent

## Visual Identity

-   Practical clothing suitable for exploration.
-   Short bob haircut.
-   Hair behaves naturally until the symbiont activates.
-   Active symbiont displays subtle biological motion and
    bioluminescence.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# The Frozen

## Role

The long-lived leadership of Skyhook civilization.

## Core Ideals

-   Preservation
-   Continuity
-   Stability
-   Long-term stewardship

They are not inherently antagonistic. Their purpose is to preserve
civilization across generations.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# The Custodians

## Role

Caretakers of the Skyhook.

They maintain:

-   Infrastructure
-   Archives
-   Life-support
-   Scientific facilities
-   Cryogenic systems
-   Surface operations

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Researchers

Scientists dedicated to understanding the Wilds through observation,
experimentation, and collaboration.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Pickers

Field specialists who recover useful equipment, biological specimens,
and unusual artifacts exposed by the movement of the Edge.

The dormant juvenile symbiont that eventually bonded with Chlora was
first recovered by Pickers.
