## Purpose

The Story Bible defines the narrative structure of Chlora.

Unlike the Canon Bible, which records established facts, this document
organizes the story, character arcs, pacing, and major revelations. It
is expected to evolve throughout development.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Scope

### This document defines

-   Narrative structure
-   Character arcs
-   Story progression
-   Major reveals
-   Themes expressed through the plot

### This document does **not** define

-   Canon facts (Canon Bible)
-   Worldbuilding details (World Bible)
-   Gameplay systems (Game Design Document)

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Narrative Premise

A young woman from the Skyhook forms an unprecedented partnership with a
juvenile symbiont, allowing her to cross the boundary between humanity
and the Wilds and gradually reshape how both worlds understand one
another.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Central Dramatic Question

Can preservation and adaptation coexist, or must one ultimately replace
the other?

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Narrative Themes

-   Curiosity over conquest
-   Understanding over fear
-   Stewardship over exploitation
-   Symbiosis over domination
-   Hope through cooperation

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Story Structure

## Before the Game

-   Humanity builds the Skyhook.
-   The Wilds remain only partially understood.
-   The Edge advances and retreats over time.
-   A dormant juvenile symbiont is recovered by Pickers.

## Act I --- The Threshold

-   Chlora bonds with the symbiont.
-   She begins exploring the Outlier.
-   She discovers that the Wilds are more complex than expected.

## Act II --- Into the Wilds

-   Chlora ventures beyond the Edge.
-   Ecological understanding becomes the primary means of progression.
-   Relationships deepen with both human and biological communities.

## Act III --- The Bridge

-   The philosophies of preservation and adaptation come into direct
    tension.
-   Chlora's unique partnership creates opportunities for cooperation
    that previously seemed impossible.

## Ending Direction

The climax should emerge from understanding rather than conquest.

The exact ending remains intentionally undefined.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Character Arc Direction

Chlora begins as an observer of the unknown.

She gradually becomes an active bridge between two civilizations,
changing not only herself but how each side perceives the other.
